in a nutshell

I earned a degree in Civil Engineering from MIT. After turning my brass rat around on graduation day, I jumped into the working world. As an operations consultant, I worked among a ton of smart and powerful people who are experts in their fields. I learned how to assess the state of a company's operations, identify its greatest opporunities for improvement, and guide clients to implement solutions that ultimately drive efficiency. The things that I learned as a consultant prepared me well for my next role as the manager of the analytics and systems-automation functions within an education non-profit. With increasing exposure to the systems that support operational functions within organizations, I came to realize that I was drawn to those systems and wanted to be able to build them for myself. Now, I have the privilege of pursuing the next chapter of my career - one that combines all of the things that I enjoy: finding creative solutions to ambiguous problems; creating efficiency and building structure out of chaos; and producing helpful tools.
